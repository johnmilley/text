# 3. the moral

The tortoise crossed the line as the hare came sprinting up behind, too
late by several lengths and one long nap. Nobody laughed at him for it —
even the animals who'd bet against him had to admit the race had been won
fairly, one steady step at a time.

The hare took it better than the others expected. "I lost because I was
certain I couldn't," he admitted. "Next time I'll run like there's no lead
to spare — even if there is."

The old fable's point survives translation after translation because it
isn't really about speed: it's about the gap between how far ahead you
*are* and how far ahead you *believe* you are, and what happens in the
distance between the two.
