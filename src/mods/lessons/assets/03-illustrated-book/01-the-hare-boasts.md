In the meadow at the foot of the hill, the animals had gathered to watch a
race that everyone but the tortoise thought was already decided. The hare
stretched, glanced down at the tortoise's short legs and heavy shell, and
laughed.

"You'd need a week's head start," the hare said, "and even then I'd nap
twice and still beat you to the oak tree."

The tortoise didn't argue. He only asked whether the hare would like to
prove it.

"Gladly," said the hare, already looking past the tortoise to the crowd,
enjoying the attention more than the challenge itself. "Tomorrow, at
sunrise. Straight line to the old oak. No shortcuts, no excuses."

The fox agreed to mark the finish. The other animals began placing bets —
all of them, without exception, on the hare.
